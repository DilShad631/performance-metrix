Key Learnings & Data-Driven Insights

A/B Test Result: Emotional Appeal Wins

The "Online Coaching" ad group test confirmed the hypothesis: messaging focused on overcoming procrastination (emotional) significantly outperformed messaging focused on AI features (logical).

Search Query Analysis: High CTR, Low Intent

The "Mock Tests" ad group had a high CTR but a very low conversion rate. The Search Query Report revealed a clear reason: the ads were attracting users with no purchase intent, who were looking for free materials.

Sample irrelevant search terms triggering ads:

"neet pg mock test free pdf" "prepladder free mock test" "neet pg previous year papers download"

Immediate Action Plan

  • Pause Losing Creatives: All "Logical Appeal" ads (Variation A) will be paused to allocate 100% of the "Coaching" budget to the winning "Emotional Hook" ads.
  • Implement Negative Keywords: Add broad match negatives (-free, -pdf, -download) to the "Mock Tests" ad group to eliminate spend on low-intent queries.
  • Re-allocate Budget: Reduce the daily budget for "Mock Tests" by 50% and re-allocate that spend to the top-performing "Online Coaching" ad group to maximize conversions.

Opportunities & Next Steps

  • Iterate on Winning Angle: Launch a new A/B test against the "Gamification" ad, testing a new emotional angle focused on "Reducing Exam Anxiety."
  • Scale What Works: If the CPA for "Online Coaching" remains below target after 72 hours, increase its daily budget by another 25% to scale lead volume.
  • Explore Landing Page Test: Propose a new landing page variant for the "Online Coaching" group that more closely mirrors the "Gamification" messaging from the winning ad.
